Shinai Accelerometry System (SAS)

I created this device to improve my kendo training during the COVID-19 pandemic. Due to local lockdown ordinances, all in-person sports were banned in my city, which was particularly troublesome for kendo practitioners, as not only was our sparring prohibited, but our practices as a whole as well. My teacher attempted to pivot to Zoom classes where students would attempt to receive feedback on their form via webcam, but this method was proven to be ineffective at best. I had seen the tech-infused training methods that other more popular sports had, so I figured that kendo could benefit from such a device as well. It was recommended that all practitioners perform suburi (practice swings) as a primary form of training, but I found this to have very little benefit. After assessing what was the most helpful component of practice that was missing during lockdown, I came to the realization that a device that could provide guidance during suburi would be helpful.

I referenced the words of my teacher heavily throughout the development process, ultimately deciding on the use of an accelerometer at the tip of the shinai as the main point of data collection. As good suburi would ideally repicate the cutting motion of a sword (swift, straight, and with a brief acceleration right before the end performed by the wrists), I used the data from the accelerometer to not only measure the amount of acceleration that the shinai's tip went through by converting the values to G's, but also checked the trajectory of the swing by analyzing the X and Y axis motion as well to determine whether the swing was "good" or "could use improvement". This info was even more useful when paired with a video recording, which would give a visual aid as to what the data represented.

Though it only developed to the point of being a rough prototype, the Shinai Accelerometry System proved to be a great aid in my lockdown kendo training, giving me the necessary information on how to continuously improve upon my skills. This enhanced training culminated in my greatly improved performance, as shown by my victory in my dojo's re-opening tournament.
